Application Overview

What is Roots?

Roots is a social network designed specifically for Northwood residents. Roots is designed to allow Northwood residents to interact with others within the Northwood community.

Who Can Use Roots?

Anyone within the Northwood community who has access to a personal computing device (smartphone, laptop, computer, tablet, etc.) and a Northwood internet connection can use Roots.

Where Can You Access Roots?

Someone can only access Roots from a Northwood Internet Connection.

Why Should You Use Roots?

Roots allows you to interact, schedule events, and communicate with your fellow residents.

Navigating To Different Pages

To access the different features of roots you must be able to navigate between the different pages.

The News Feed

 

What is the News Feed?

The news feed is a One to All messaging system. This allows you to read and post messages that all other Users will be able to see. The news feed is ordered by newest post at the top of the page

Viewing the News Feed

You can view the news feed from the log in page, thus you don’t need an account to view the news feed. From a signed in account you can view the news feed within the “News Feed” tab. To view older posts scroll downwards on the news feed.

 

Creating a Post

To create a post you must be signed in to a Roots Account. Using the window shown below you can type a message. Once it is ready to be posted simply press the complete button and it will be posted for all users to see.

Events

What is an Event?

An event is similar to a post however it adds additional functionality. Events represents events that are occurring at a particular time and date.

 

View Events

You can view events only from a logged in account. To view the events feed navigate to the “Events” tab. Events are ordered based upon what is happening next. Thus newest events are placed at the top of the feed.

Creating an Event

To create an event, first navigate to the events tab. Then press the “Create an Event” button. Then fill in the information required to make the event, see photo below. Then click the submit button.

Interact With an Event

Interacting with an event can inform the creator you intend on going to the event. This allows the creator if the event should take place.

Mail

What is Mail?

Mail is a one to one messaging system, similar to an email system. You can start a conversation, reply to a user, and delete personal messages.

Reading Your Mail

If you receive a piece of mail you will see a notification count above the “Mail” tab. Navigate to the mail tab and press read on the unread message to view the message.

Starting a Conversation

To start a conversation navigate to the “Mail” tab. Then click “Start a Conversation”. Then fill recipients name, the subject line, and the message content. When the message is completed press the “Send” button and the message will be sent to the recipient’s inbox.

Replying to a Message

If you receive a message from another user and wish to reply to the message. Press the “Reply” button. You will then be prompted with a windows to type your response. Once you have completed your response, pressing the “Send” button will send your reply to the sender.

Deleting a Message

After you read a message and do not wish to respond to the message and don’t want it sitting in your inbox you can press the “Delete” button to remove the message from your inbox.
